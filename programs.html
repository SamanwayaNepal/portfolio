Our work

Eight strategic program areas.

Each pillar is drawn directly from our statutory mandate — together they form a complete framework for youth-led democratic change.

1

Democratic Governance & Civic Engagement

Sustainable pathways for youth engagement in public administration — from municipal ward levels to national policy consultations. Civic literacy modules, mock parliaments, and youth-government roundtables.

2

Social Inclusion, Equity & Justice

Amplifying the leadership pipelines of Dalit communities, indigenous Janajatis, women, persons with disabilities, and underserved populations by centering their localized priorities.

3

Leadership Incubation & Capacity Building

Specialized tracks in public-policy fluency, project management, and ethical decision-making — combining formal skill-building with experiential community organizing.

4

Human Rights & Rule of Law

Evidence-based accountability tracking across all three tiers of federal government: procurement monitoring, RTI implementation, legal literacy, and community-led documentation.

5

Non-Partisan Deliberation & Collaboration

Secure, non-partisan arenas that bridge political, ethnic, and regional polarization through multi-stakeholder symposia and habits of systematic listening and consensus-building.

6

Climate Action & Environmental Sustainability

Community-based ecosystem conservation, municipal climate-adaptation frameworks, green-economy advocacy, and youth-led climate-justice campaigns.

7

Digital Literacy & Applied Research

Bridging the digital divide through technology bootcamps and e-governance tracking, alongside rigorous social-science research and policy briefs.

8

Livelihoods, Culture & Community Development

Modern agricultural extension, cooperative supply chains, and public health awareness — while documenting indigenous knowledge and multi-ethnic performing arts.
